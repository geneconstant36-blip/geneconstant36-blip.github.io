E-READ Initiative - what is it

The E-READ Initiative (which stands for Evolution of Reading in the Age of Digitalisation) was a major interdisciplinary research project funded by the European COST Action (IS1404) between 2014 and 2018. It involved over 200 scholars and scientists across Europe investigating how digital technologies affect our reading habits and comprehension.

Key Focus & Findings
The initiative was primarily concerned with the "digital disadvantage"—the phenomenon where reading on screens can lead to lower comprehension compared to reading on paper. Their research, culminating in the Stavanger Declaration (2019), highlighted several critical findings:

Comprehension Gap: Readers generally perform better on paper for long, complex texts. When reading on screens, people tend to skim more and are more likely to be overconfident about how much they have actually understood.

The "Scrolling" Effect: Scrolling through text rather than turning pages can increase cognitive load, making it harder to build a mental map of the information.

Digital Natives Myth: The study debunked the idea that younger "digital natives" are naturally better at digital reading. Even for younger generations, paper remains the superior medium for deep immersion and retention.
